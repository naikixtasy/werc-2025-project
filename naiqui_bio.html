Naiqui Armendariz

Aerospace & Mechanical Engineering Student | Team Lead | Researcher | Aspiring Astronaut

Naiqui Armendariz is a senior double-majoring in Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering at New Mexico State University. With a profound passion for space exploration and sustainable innovation, she brings over a decade of multidisciplinary experience across IT, research, CAD modeling, and advanced manufacturing.

As Team Lead for the WERC 2025 project, she guided system design and integration for a renewable energy-powered water purification system with real-time soil monitoring, leveraging her expertise in SolidWorks, CATIA, and hands-on electronics.

Naiqui is the founder of Geekland 3D, a small business offering custom 3D printing services that integrate engineering design, precision prototyping, and additive manufacturing techniques. She is also currently working in IT at the Burrell College of Osteopathic Medicine and has interned at Supernal, where she developed sensor integration systems for autonomous flight platforms.

Her research experience includes projects under NASA's Student Spaceflight Experiments Program and the RISE program, investigating water purification using cactus mucilage in microgravity. She also mentored international teams for the Martian Greenhouse 3.0 initiative.

Naiqui is fluent in English and Spanish and is conversant in Japanese, French, and Italian. She is certified in SOLIDWORKS and CATIA and proficient in coding with Python, Arduino (C/C++), ROS (Robot Operating System), and basic Linux/Ubuntu terminal skills.

Her ultimate goal is to contribute to humanity's presence in space and inspire future generations through education and ethical engineering.
