Overview

Renewable Energy Solution for Water & Environmental Restoration is a multidisciplinary engineering project developed for the WERC 2025 Environmental Design Contest. The project addresses the increasing risk of erosion, debris contamination, and long-term soil damage in wildfire-affected ecosystems by offering a scalable, off-grid water purification and soil recovery system.

Our solution integrates a wind-powered pump with a natural media filtration column and real-time soil moisture monitoring system. The system is designed to:

  • Collect contaminated water from simulated post-fire environments.
  • Filter it through a multi-layer natural media column consisting of sand, cotton, activated coconut charcoal, lava rock, and gravel‚Äîremoving turbidity and impurities.
  • Return clean water to the environment for rehydration of burned soil.
  • Monitor soil conditions in real time using capacitive moisture sensors, controlled by an ESP8266 (Arduino-compatible) system, with data logging to an external interface.

Unlike traditional post-fire interventions, our system is completely powered by renewable energy (wind, with direct sunlight backup), requires minimal infrastructure, and is designed for modular deployment in remote and rugged terrains.

This project demonstrates how environmental engineering, sustainability, and embedded systems can be combined into a practical, low-cost solution to accelerate soil and water restoration from a 20‚Äì25 year process to just 5‚Äì10 years.
